Notes on Orientation

Configure your pre-orientation settings for consistent scrambling.

If one has the following pre-orientation settings:

Starting from WCA Orientation

Follow this sequence before applying the scramble:

1. First do a rotation
2. Then a random number of rotations
3. Finally a random number of rotations

To ensure your scrambled cube matches that of the one displayed on screen of the trainer, apply the first rotation (e.g., z2) to your cube from the default orientation (White top, Green front) before applying the scramble.

For the standard Western color scheme, z2 means starting with Yellow top, Green front, instead of White Top, Green Front. This is ideal for CFOP solvers who solve on white cross but want to train cases from the yellow-top perspective.

The second and third boxes define Color Neutrality. In this example, random x2 and y rotations occur before each scramble. For Roux solvers, this corresponds to x2/y neutrality.

Note: For many algsets, rotating before the scramble doesn't affect how difficult a case can be recognzed. You can leave the random boxes blank to focus on a single orientation. The random orientation is perhaps more useful for virtual cube training, where all matters of preorientation are handled automatically.
